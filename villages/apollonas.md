# Apollonas Village, Naxos

Apollonas is a small fishing village on the north coast of Naxos, 36 km from Chora. Known for the giant Kouros statue above the village and one of the best fish lunches on the island.

## Character

- Traditional fishing harbour with painted wooden boats
- A handful of excellent fish tavernas on the waterfront
- Small sandy beach suitable for swimming
- Much quieter than the west coast resorts

## Highlights

- **Kouros of Apollonas** — 10.5-metre unfinished archaic statue in the quarry above the village (8th century BC), free, open site
- **Fish lunch** — the tavernas in Apollonas serve fresh local catch; grilled octopus and sea bream are the specialities
- **North coast road** — the drive to Apollonas along the mountain road is one of the most scenic on the island
