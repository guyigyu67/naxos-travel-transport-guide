# Halki Village, Naxos

Halki (also written Chalki) is a beautifully preserved neoclassical village in the heart of the Tragea plain, 16 km from Chora. It was once the capital of Naxos.

## Character

Halki feels like a living museum of Naxian wealth and history. Grand 19th-century neoclassical mansions line the lanes alongside Byzantine-era churches. The village is quiet, unhurried, and free from the beach-resort tourism of the coast.

## Highlights

- **Vallindras Distillery** — the oldest kitron distillery in Naxos, operating since 1896. Kitron is a liqueur made from the leaves of the citron tree (Citrus medica), unique to Naxos. Free tasting available.
- **Panagia Protothrone Church** — 9th-century Byzantine church, one of the oldest on the island
- **Venetian Tower of Barozzi-Grazia** — fortified tower rising above the village square
- **Tragea plain** — surrounding olive groves, a fertile valley with walking paths between villages
