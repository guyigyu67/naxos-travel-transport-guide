# Temple of Demeter, Naxos

The Temple of Demeter is one of the best-preserved ancient Greek temples in the Cyclades, located in the village of Sangri, 13 km from Chora.

## History

Built around 530–520 BC and dedicated to Demeter, goddess of harvest and fertility. Constructed from fine Naxian marble. When Christianity spread to the Cyclades, the temple was converted into a Christian basilica — a small church was built inside its walls. This unusual layering of religious history is preserved in the site today.

## Visiting

- **Location:** Gyroulas hill, near Ano Sangri village, 13 km from Chora
- **Opening hours:** Tuesday–Sunday, 08:30–15:30 (check locally for seasonal changes)
- **Entry fee:** Small admission fee
- **Duration:** 45–60 min including the small on-site museum
