# Kouros of Apollonas, Naxos

The Kouros of Apollonas is a colossal ancient Greek statue lying unfinished in an open-air quarry above the village of Apollonas, in the north of Naxos.

## What it is

A kouros is an archaic Greek statue of a standing male figure, typically depicting a young man or a god. The Apollonas kouros dates from the 8th century BC and measures 10.5 metres — one of the largest kouroi in Greece. It was abandoned in the quarry when a crack appeared during carving. The site gives a unique insight into ancient Greek sculpting methods.

## Visiting

- **Location:** Above Apollonas village, 36 km from Chora
- **Access:** Free, open site
- **Duration:** 20–30 min at the statue, plus time in Apollonas village
- **Combine with:** The Melanes kouros (6 km from Chora, smaller, also unfinished) for a full "kouroi tour"

## Getting there

Apollonas is 36 km from Chora via the mountain road. No direct bus. The journey through the north is scenic but takes 45–50 min each way.
