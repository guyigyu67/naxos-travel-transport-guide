# The Portara — Temple of Apollo, Naxos

The Portara is the most iconic landmark in Naxos: a giant marble doorway standing alone on the small islet of Palatia, connected to the port by a causeway.

## What it is

The Portara is the surviving gate of an unfinished Temple of Apollo, begun around 530 BC under the tyrant Lygdamis. Construction was abandoned — possibly due to political upheaval — and the temple was never completed. The four marble blocks forming the doorway each weigh around 20 tonnes.

## Visiting

- **Location:** Islet of Palatia, 10-min walk from Naxos Port along the causeway
- **Access:** Free, open at all hours
- **Best time:** Sunset — the Portara frames the sun setting over Paros, one of the most photographed moments in the Cyclades
- **Duration:** 20–30 min

## Getting there
