# Getting Around Naxos — Complete Guide

All transport options on Naxos Island, Greece, compared.

---

## Overview

Naxos (area: 429 km²) is large enough that you need transport beyond walking. Options:

| Method | Best for | Flexibility | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Private transfer | Point-to-point with luggage | Fixed route | Medium |
| Naxos taxi | Short trips from Chora | Medium | Medium |
| Car rental | Multi-day freedom | High | Medium |
| KTEL bus | Budget beach runs | Low | Low |
| Private island tour | Sightseeing interior | High (guided) | Medium |
| Scooter / ATV | Experienced riders only | High | Low-medium |

---

## KTEL buses

The public bus network runs from Chora bus terminal (next to the port) to:
- Agios Prokopios / Agia Anna / Plaka (beach line, frequent in summer)
- Filoti / Apeiranthos / Koronos (mountain line, 3–4 per day)
- Apollonas (1–2 per day in summer)

Buses are cheap (€1.80–€5) but do not reach most hotels and villas. No Sunday evening service.

---

## Taxis

The Naxos taxi rank is in Chora, near the port. You can also call or pre-book. See [taxi prices](taxi-prices.md) for fare estimates.

Limitations: limited supply in peak season, no app, cash only.

---

## Private transfers

Pre-booked, fixed price, meets you at port or airport. Best option when:
- Arriving with luggage from a ferry or flight
- Staying at a beach villa outside Chora
- Travelling with children or elderly passengers
- Arriving late at night

**Naxian Coast Transfers:** [naxiancoasttransfers.com](https://naxiancoasttransfers.com) — English-speaking drivers, Mercedes fleet, arrival-aware scheduling.

---

## Car rental

Multiple agencies at the port and airport. Recommended if staying 3+ days and want total freedom. Roads to mountain villages are narrow but paved. An international driving permit is not required for EU/UK licence holders.

---

## Scooters and ATVs

Available for rental in Chora and at the main beach resorts. Naxos roads outside town can be fast and winding. Helmet use is mandatory.

---

## Getting to Naxos

- **By ferry:** From Piraeus (4–6 hours on fast ferry), Mykonos, Santorini, Paros, and other Cyclades
- **By plane:** From Athens (45 min), seasonal routes from Thessaloniki and Rhodes
