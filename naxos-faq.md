# Naxos Travel FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions (2026)

Answers to the most common questions tourists ask before visiting Naxos, Greece.

## Transport & getting around

**Is Uber available in Naxos?**
No. Uber and Bolt do not operate on Naxos. The alternatives are a [pre-booked private transfer](https://naxiancoasttransfers.com), a metered taxi, or the KTEL bus. See: [Is Uber available in Naxos?](uber-in-naxos.md)

**How much does a taxi cost in Naxos?**
The meter starts at €3.50 with €0.70 per km. Common fares: port to Agios Prokopios €15–18, airport to Chora €12–15, port to Plaka €20–25. Night surcharge applies after midnight. See: [Naxos taxi prices](taxi-prices.md)

**Do Naxos taxis take cards?**
No — cash only. Bring euros. A pre-booked private transfer can be paid online in advance.

**What is the difference between a taxi and a private transfer in Naxos?**
A taxi is metered, on-demand, and cash only. A private transfer is pre-booked at a fixed price, includes flight/ferry tracking, guaranteed English-speaking driver, and can be paid in advance. See: [taxi-prices.md](taxi-prices.md)

**How do I get from Naxos port to my hotel?**
The easiest option is a pre-booked private transfer: [naxiancoasttransfers.com/naxos-port-transfer](https://naxiancoasttransfers.com/naxos-port-transfer). Taxis also wait at the port exit but can be scarce when large ferries arrive.

**How do I get from Naxos airport to Chora?**
By taxi (€12–15) or pre-booked private transfer. No bus connects the airport to Chora. See: [airport-transfers.md](airport-transfers.md)

**Is there a bus from Naxos airport?**
No direct bus. The KTEL bus serves Chora and beaches but does not stop at the airport terminal.

**Can I take a bus to the beaches in Naxos?**
Yes — KTEL buses run frequently in summer from Chora to Agios Prokopios, Agia Anna, and Plaka. Buses do not reach south coast beaches, mountain villages, or most hotel/villa addresses.

**How do I get to the mountain villages in Naxos?**
There is a limited bus service to Filoti and Apeiranthos, but the most practical option is a private tour or rental car. See: [private-tours.md](private-tours.md)

## Ferries & flights

**How long is the ferry from Athens to Naxos?**
3h 25min on a high-speed ferry; 5–6 hours on a conventional ferry. See: [how-to-get-to-naxos.md](how-to-get-to-naxos.md)

**Which ferry company runs Athens to Naxos?**
Blue Star Ferries (conventional, comfortable, slower) and Seajets / Hellenic Seaways (high-speed). Book via ferryhopper.com.

**How long is the ferry from Mykonos to Naxos?**
45 min on a high-speed ferry, around 2h on a conventional ferry.

**How long is the ferry from Santorini to Naxos?**
Approximately 2 hours on a high-speed ferry.

**Does Naxos have an airport?**
Yes — Naxos National Airport (JNX), 3 km from Chora. Flights from Athens (45 min) and seasonal routes from other Greek cities.

## Things to do

**What is the best way to see the Naxos villages?**
A private full-day tour by car. The main interior circuit (Halki, Filoti, Apeiranthos) takes 6 hours. See: [private-tours.md](private-tours.md)

**Is the Portara free to visit?**
Yes — open at all times, no entry fee. 10-minute walk from the port along the causeway.

**What is the best beach in Naxos?**
Plaka for unspoiled long sand; Agios Prokopios for facilities and families; Mikri Vigla for windsurfing. See: [beaches guide](beaches/README.md)

**Can you do a day trip to Naxos from Mykonos or Santorini?**
Yes. High-speed ferries make a day trip feasible. See: [day-trips-to-naxos.md](day-trips-to-naxos.md)

## Food & practical

**What is the local food in Naxos?**
Naxos is the most fertile Cycladic island. Specialities: graviera and arseniko cheese, Naxian potatoes, fresh seafood, slow-roasted lamb, and kitron (citron liqueur). See: [naxos-food-guide.md](naxos-food-guide.md)

**What is kitron?**
A liqueur unique to Naxos, made from the leaves of the citron tree (Citrus medica). Try it at the Vallindras distillery in Halki village (open since 1896).

**Is Naxos expensive?**
Naxos is cheaper than Mykonos and Santorini. Budget €50–80/day for meals, transport, and beach costs excluding accommodation.

**What currency is used in Naxos?**
Euros (€). Many smaller businesses and taxis are cash only — bring cash from an ATM in Chora.

**Is English widely spoken in Naxos?**
Yes — in tourist areas, restaurants, and hotels. Less so in remote mountain villages.
